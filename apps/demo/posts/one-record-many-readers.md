When you publish a page, you publish a destination: one URL, on one server,
rendered one way. When you publish a record instead, you publish the thing
itself — a small, structured fact that anyone can read and render however
they like. The page becomes just one view of it.

That is the quiet shift behind a standard.site document. This very post is a
record sitting in my repo on the open network. My site renders it into the
page you're reading, but a feed reader, an archive, or someone else's client
could render the same record on their own terms, and none of them need my
permission or my server to do it.

Owning your website used to mean owning the server. Increasingly it means
owning the record — the canonical copy of what you said — and letting the
rendering be everyone's to share. The server can go down; the record is
still yours.
